# Auto Radar Research Method

## Purpose

Auto Radar is not only a dashboard or trading tool.

It is a research system designed to continuously improve decision quality through evidence, validation, failure review, and model correction.

The goal is not to predict every market move.

The goal is to reduce uncertainty and improve long-term expected value.

---

## Core Principle

Every decision is a market experiment.

Every result must become knowledge.

A win increases confidence only if the reasoning was correct.

A loss becomes valuable if it reveals a missing factor, wrong assumption, excessive confidence, or incorrect weight.

---

## Research Cycle

Each decision follows this cycle:

1. Hypothesis
2. Evidence
3. Decision
4. Outcome
5. Root Cause Analysis
6. Lesson
7. Weight Update
8. Retest

---

## 1. Hypothesis

A hypothesis is a testable market claim.

Examples:

* If Micron earnings strongly beat expectations and SOX confirms, Memory theme should outperform over the next 1 to 5 trading days.
* If a major crash occurs but AI-era investors quickly frame it as a buy-the-dip setup, the selloff duration may be shorter than historical crashes.
* If a pattern is highly known by the market, its future excess return may decline.

A hypothesis must include:

* Trigger
* Expected effect
* Time horizon
* Required evidence
* Failure condition

---

## 2. Evidence

Evidence must be separated into layers:

* Event: What happened?
* Expectation: Was it expected or surprising?
* Market: How did index / futures / sector react?
* Capital: Did real money confirm?
* Theme: Which themes are affected?
* Leader: Did key stocks confirm?
* Technical: Did price / volume / breadth confirm?
* History: What happened in similar cases?
* Cognition: Has the market already learned this pattern?

No Buy / Watch / Wait / Avoid decision should rely on a single evidence layer.

---

## 3. Decision

Every decision must be explicit:

* Buy
* Watch
* Wait
* Avoid

Every decision must include:

* Decision Score
* Confidence
* Risk level
* Evidence summary
* Invalid condition
* Suggested exposure range

Auto Radar may provide a direction, but final risk belongs to 綠茶 CEO.

---

## 4. Outcome

Outcome must be tracked after predefined windows:

* 1 day
* 3 days
* 5 days
* 10 days
* 20 days

The result must include:

* Return
* Max drawdown
* Max favorable move
* Whether thesis was validated
* Whether timing was correct

A profitable trade with wrong reasoning is not a full success.

A losing trade with correct reasoning but an external shock is not a full failure.

---

## 5. Root Cause Analysis

Every failed or weak decision must answer:

1. What did we miss?
2. What did we overestimate?
3. What did we underestimate?
4. Which evidence layer failed?
5. Was the event already priced in?
6. Did capital flow disagree?
7. Did market psychology shift?
8. Did AI / information diffusion accelerate market reaction?
9. Was the pattern saturated?
10. Should we adjust weights, add a factor, or remove a rule?

---

## 6. Lesson

A Lesson is the permanent output of review.

Examples:

* Strong event without capital confirmation has low success rate.
* A well-known crash-buying pattern may shorten selloff duration.
* High awareness reduces future alpha.
* AI-driven information diffusion may compress reaction time.
* Stage Score should not be upgraded by event impact alone without price / volume confirmation.

Every Lesson should be traceable to one or more decisions.

---

## 7. Weight Update

Weights are not changed because of one opinion.

Weights are changed only when repeated evidence shows a factor is overpowered, underpowered, or no longer useful.

Examples:

* If Event Score repeatedly gives false positives, reduce event weight.
* If Capital Flow consistently predicts success, increase capital weight.
* If Pattern Saturation predicts failure, add saturation penalty.

---

## 8. Retest

After modifying a rule, Auto Radar must retest it against future cases.

A rule is never considered final.

The market changes, and therefore Auto Radar must change with it.

---

## Research Standard

Any new factor must answer:

1. Why should this improve decision quality?
2. How can it be measured?
3. How can it fail?
4. How will we know if it works?
5. When should we remove it?

If a factor cannot be measured, reviewed, or invalidated, it should remain a research note and not enter the core model.

---

## Final Rule

Auto Radar does not collect trades.

Auto Radar accumulates market knowledge.
