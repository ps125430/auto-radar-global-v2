# Market Learning Engine

## Purpose

The Market Learning Engine studies how the market learns, adapts, forgets, and changes its behavior over repeated exposure to similar events.

The core belief:

The market is not a fixed opponent.

The market also learns.

Therefore, historical patterns do not stay equally useful forever.

---

## Core Question

For every event and pattern, Auto Radar must ask:

Has the market already learned this?

If yes, the pattern may be saturated, priced in, or vulnerable to reversal.

If no, the pattern may still have edge.

---

## Market Learning Cycle

A market pattern usually evolves through stages:

1. Unknown
2. Discovered by a few
3. Adopted by early participants
4. Widely known
5. Crowded
6. Front-run
7. Reversed / exploited
8. Forgotten

The same event can produce different market reactions depending on where it sits in this cycle.

---

## Key Concepts

### 1. Market Memory

Market Memory measures whether investors remember how similar events behaved in the past.

Example:

COVID crash in 2020 taught many investors that panic selloffs can become major buying opportunities.

Future crashes may therefore be bought faster than the first one.

---

### 2. Expectation

Expectation measures what the market already believed before the event.

The true impact of an event is:

Event Result - Market Expectation

A good event can be bearish if it was already fully priced in.

A bad event can be bullish if the market expected worse.

---

### 3. Awareness Score

Awareness Score measures how widely known a pattern is.

High Awareness:

* Many people know the setup
* Media and social platforms discuss it heavily
* AI tools likely explain it similarly
* Excess return may decline

Low Awareness:

* Few participants recognize the setup
* The market may not have fully priced it in
* Potential edge may be higher

---

### 4. Pattern Saturation

Pattern Saturation measures whether a pattern has become overcrowded.

A saturated pattern may show:

* Fast price reaction
* Weak follow-through
* Open-high-close-low behavior
* More fake breakouts
* More reversal after obvious signals

---

### 5. Information Diffusion

Information Diffusion measures how fast information spreads.

AI and social platforms may compress market reaction time.

In the past, a major event could take days to digest.

In the AI era, a similar event may be digested in minutes or hours.

---

### 6. AI Influence

AI Influence measures whether AI tools are likely changing market behavior.

If many investors ask AI systems similar questions and receive similar answers, market behavior may become more synchronized.

This can create:

* Faster reaction
* More crowded trades
* Shorter trend duration
* More violent reversals

AI is not only a tool.

AI can become a market factor.

---

### 7. Adaptation Score

Adaptation Score measures whether the market has changed its behavior toward a specific event type.

Example:

If the first crash produced panic selling but later crashes were bought quickly, the market has adapted.

---

## Signals to Observe

The Market Learning Engine should observe:

* Event repetition count
* Time since last similar event
* Market reaction speed
* Selloff duration
* Rebound speed
* Volume behavior
* Breadth recovery
* Leader stock response
* Media / social / AI discussion intensity
* Whether price moved before the event
* Whether price reversed after the event

---

## Research Hypotheses

Initial hypotheses:

1. The more familiar a pattern becomes, the faster the market reacts.
2. The faster the market reacts, the shorter the tradable edge window becomes.
3. When Awareness is high, obvious signals produce more fakeouts.
4. AI may increase market synchronization and compress reaction time.
5. Major selloffs may recover faster if investors believe they are repeating a known buy-the-dip playbook.
6. A pattern can regain edge after the market forgets it.

These are hypotheses, not permanent rules.

They must be validated.
