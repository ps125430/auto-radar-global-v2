# Hypothesis Framework

## Purpose

The Hypothesis Framework prevents Auto Radar from turning intuition into permanent rules too early.

Every new idea must first become a testable hypothesis.

Only validated hypotheses can become model rules.

---

## Hypothesis Template

Each hypothesis must include:

```markdown
# Hypothesis H0001

## Title

## Claim

## Why It Might Work

## Trigger Conditions

## Expected Outcome

## Time Horizon

## Evidence Needed

## Failure Conditions

## Data Needed

## Review Schedule

## Status
Research / Testing / Validated / Rejected / Archived
```

---

## Example Hypothesis

# Hypothesis H0001

## Title

AI-era investors may shorten crash duration.

## Claim

When a major market crash resembles a familiar historical buy-the-dip playbook, investors may buy faster than in earlier cycles, causing the decline to be shorter and rebounds to be sharper.

## Why It Might Work

Market participants remember COVID-era crash recovery, and AI tools may accelerate the spread of historical comparison.

## Trigger Conditions

* Major index drawdown > 5%
* High media fear
* Similarity to prior crash playbook
* Rapid AI / social discussion of buy-the-dip narrative

## Expected Outcome

Selloff duration shortens versus comparable historical events.

## Time Horizon

1 to 10 trading days

## Evidence Needed

* Index drawdown
* Rebound speed
* Volume behavior
* VIX reaction
* Social / media / AI narrative proxy
* Capital flow confirmation

## Failure Conditions

* Selloff continues beyond historical average
* Capital exits aggressively
* Credit / liquidity shock appears
* Macro event is structurally worse than prior playbook

## Status

Research

---

## Hypothesis Lifecycle

1. Research
2. Testing
3. Validated
4. Integrated
5. Decayed
6. Rejected
7. Archived

---

## Validation Rules

A hypothesis should not become a model rule until it has:

* Clear data definition
* At least several comparable cases
* Recorded successes and failures
* Known failure modes
* Weight adjustment logic
* Review owner

---

## Rejection Rules

A hypothesis should be rejected if:

* It cannot be measured
* It cannot be invalidated
* It depends only on vague feeling
* It repeatedly fails in real cases
* It creates false confidence
* It does not improve decision quality

---

## Core Principle

A good hypothesis is allowed to be wrong.

A bad hypothesis is impossible to test.
